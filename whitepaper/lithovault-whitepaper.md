# LithoVault Yellow Paper

**Title:** LithoVault — Autonomous Treasury Infrastructure for Lithosphere  
**Subtitle:** A production architecture for autonomous corporate finance, AI-agent treasury operations, DNNS identity, APP payment rails, and MultX cross-chain treasury execution.  
**Version:** 0.9 draft  
**Network:** Lithosphere Makalu (Chain ID 700777)  

## Abstract
LithoVault is an autonomous CFO and treasury system designed to let enterprises and sophisticated users manage on-chain capital through governed AI execution. The system combines policy-controlled vaults, AI decision pipelines, multi-signature governance, DNNS-based agent identity, APP payment rails, and MultX cross-chain treasury coordination.

## 1. Introduction
Treasury operations are becoming software-native. Yield capture, inventory rotation, stablecoin buffering, execution routing, and risk containment increasingly occur on decentralized rails. LithoVault formalizes those workflows as auditable, governable state machines.

## 2. Problem Statement
Manual treasury management suffers from latency, fragmented execution, and inconsistent controls. Human treasury teams cannot continuously observe market conditions, execute optimal reallocations, or preserve high-fidelity audit trails at machine speed.

## 3. Design Goals
1. Deterministic on-chain controls.
2. Human-readable agent identity.
3. Cross-chain treasury coordination.
4. Policy-bounded AI execution.
5. Enterprise reporting and operational resilience.

## 4. System Overview
LithoVault is organized into six planes: governance, treasury vaults, agent intelligence, identity, cross-chain coordination, and observability.

## 5. Lithosphere Integration
The reference deployment targets Lithosphere Makalu using chain ID 700777 and RPC endpoint https://rpc.litho.ai.

## 6. APP Payment Rail Integration
APP serves as the payment routing substrate for internal treasury job execution, harvest scheduling, and cost attribution.

## 7. DNNS Identity
DNNS names bind treasury agents and policy domains to verifiable controller records and zk-linked commitments.

## 8. MultX Coordination
MultX enables multi-validator cross-chain relay patterns, making it suitable for treasury instructions that must be observed, validated, relayed, and finalized across chains.

## 9. Vault State Model
A vault is defined by asset balances, policy envelopes, strategy permissions, execution queue state, and operator attestations.

## 10. Governance Model
Governance combines board-level signers, policy thresholds, time-locks, and emergency pause semantics.

## 11. AI and Quantt Pipelines
Quantt pipelines collect market, vault, and risk signals and feed multiple model adapters. Votes are normalized into a consensus action with traceable reasoning.

## 12. ZK Identity Layer
Each agent can publish a commitment and optionally prove membership in an accepted root set without exposing underlying identity material.

## 13. Cross-Chain Treasury Bridge
Bridge instructions are encoded as treasury intents, signed by policy-authorized actors, and relayed through a MultX-compatible bridge adapter.

## 14. Security Model
Primary controls include signer separation, policy enforcement, circuit breakers, simulation, threshold approvals, and isolated execution adapters.

## 15. Compliance and Auditability
LithoVault emits structured events and machine-readable reports suitable for accounting, board review, and forensic reconstruction.

## 16. Reference Deployment
The reference stack includes Next.js frontend, Expo mobile app, Fastify API, Hardhat contracts, TS/Python SDKs, CLI tools, Postgres, and Redis.

## 17. Roadmap
Near-term work includes production bridge hardening, proofs integration, strategy backtesting, execution safety checks, and ERP connectors.
